Kasol

WHAT CAN I EXPECT FROM KASOL

Kasol is a beautiful hamlet which reflects Israeli culture, you can even get great variety of genuine Israeli food as well. It is also called as the little Israel of India. Kasol is surrounded by snow capped mountains and elegant pine forests. It resembles much the beauty of molten gold in the first and last beams of sunlight.

ACTIVITIES IN KASOL

As there are lots of things to do in Kasol during your travel and trip. Some of the most popular things to do in Kasol, Himachal Pradesh: Hiking: There are numerous trails that lead into the mountains that surround Kasol, and it is simple to find your way to beautiful waterfalls, small villages, and other attractions. There are also several beautiful walks through town, including one along the river that leads to a Tibetan refugee village. Kheerganga trek is one of the best treks around the Parvati River that can be done all year. Local Village: Kasol is surrounded by beautiful small villages where many locals live. There is plenty of opportunity for sightseeing, and it can be a great way to meet new people and learn more about the local culture. Kasol offers a variety of activities. You can also pay a small fee to get a tour from a local who will point out important places such as the homes of famous Tibetan translators and meditation temples. Trekking: Kasol has several trekking options that are open all year and provide breathtaking views of the town, nearby mountains, and villages like Malana, Tosh, and Rassol. The hikes are strenuous and will take several days to complete, but the locals have used them for generations. A trip to Kasol can be an incredible experience if you want to learn more about the local culture or the Sikhs and Buddhists. Yoga: Locals have been using Kasol's hot springs for years, trying to keep them as natural as possible by adding tiny pollutants or pollutants. The hot springs are now open to all visitors and can be marveled at when they are flowing during the warmer seasons. It is still possible to soak in these gushing hot springs during the winter, but swimming is not recommended due to the freezing water temperatures.
